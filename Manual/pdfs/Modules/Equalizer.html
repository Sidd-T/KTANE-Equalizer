On the Subject of Equalizer

Write music eat soap

  • To solve this module, find appropriate settings for the Equalizer (EQ)
  • This EQ has three frequency 'bands': low, mid, and high.
  • Each band represents a range of frequency between 10 Hz to 10000 Hz
  • The EQ has controls for the gain of each band, the top of the low band, the bottom of the high band, and a choice for 24 dB or 48 dB roll-off
  • EQ'ing is done to bring out the sound of a certain instrument, which must be determined along with a genre.
  • Once appropriate settings are input, press "Bypass" to submit the answer.
  • An wrong answer yields a strike. The correct answer remains unchanged.
  • Other necessary info is found in Appendix EQ3: Instrument & EQ Data.

Determining the Instrument

The instrument can be determined as follows:

If the bomb has a parallel port, the instrument is Piano.
Otherwise, if there is a lit FRK or CAR indicator, the instrument is Piccolo.
Otherwise, if the serial number has any of "VIOLN", the instrument is Violin.
Otherwise, check the increment used in any of the gain screens:

  • If the increment is 1, the instrument is Bass.
  • Otherwise, the instrument is Drums.

Determining the Genre

The genre is determined based on the instrument. Once the instrument has been found, use the following table to determine the genre. Use the number of batteries on the bomb to determine what row of the table to search. Some instruments do not have a genre and are EQ'd similarily across genres, indicated by "N/A" in the table.

Piano Piccolo Violin Bass Drums
0 or 1 Jazz N/A N/A EDM Rock
2 or 3 Anime N/A N/A Jazz Trap
4+ Classical N/A N/A Rock Jazz

Equalization

Once you have determined the instrument and genre, follow the rules below to input appropriate settings. Remember to use Appendix EQ3: Instrument & EQ Data.

  • Set the low frequency and/or high frequency split(s) to emcompass the instrument's average frequency in the correct band.

  • If the instrument's average frequency is in the Mid band, do not mute the Low and High bands, and keep their gains lower than the Mid band.

  • Then, adjust the gains of the three bands so that the main band encompassing the instrument's average frequency is the highest. Then, based on the genre, adjust the other bands according to the chart below.

  • If the high band is cut, use the 48 dB filter roll-off, otherwise use 24 dB.
  • Remember that bands do not have to be exact, but only have to contain the frequencies required
Instrument Genre Cut Secondary Band Secondary Band Gain
Piano Jazz None Low, below 200 Hz -3 dB from High Band
Anime Low, below 250 Hz High, above 1400 Hz < 3 dB difference from Mid
Classical None None None
Piccolo None Low, below 500 Hz Mid, above 500 Hz < 5 dB difference from High
Violin None None None None
Bass EDM High, above 1600 Hz Mid -4 dB from Low
Jazz High, above 2100 Hz Mid < 3 dB difference from Low
Rock High, above 2100 Hz Mid < 4 dB difference from Low
Drums Rock High, above 2000 Hz Mid, below 2000 Hz < 4 dB difference from Low
Trap High, above 1800 Hz Mid -4 dB from Low
